This network graph shows the common language used to describe UFO encounters. It’s drawn directly from comments made by individuals during their sightings, offering us an unfiltered lens into their experiences.

In constructing the graph, I purposefully excluded everyday, filler words such as "it", "a", "has", and so forth. By doing so, the spotlight falls onto more descriptive, impactful words used to narrate these sightings.

What the graph uncovers is genuinely intriguing. It points to recurring two-word combinations - or bigrams - like "light/lights", "green", "red", "sky moving", and others. These words, frequently cropping up in descriptions, give us a glimpse into how most people characterize their UFO encounters.

What's more, there's a noticeable overlap in these descriptions. This pattern might suggest that observers, whether they're actually witnessing an authentic UFO or perhaps misidentifying a military fighter jet, are generally seeing similar phenomena in the skies.
